This is a demo negotiation of the chatbot used in my behavioral experiments on human–AI negotiation (see the technical paper). You take the role of a Supplier; the Retailer is an AI agent. Its offer acceptance and counter-offer decisions are rule-based; its chat messages are generated by an LLM. Chat is non-binding: only offers sent through the interface, or confirmed there, are binding. No data is collected on this page.

The setting

You negotiate a contract on the wholesale price w and the order quantity q for widgets. The Production Cost is PC = 1.00€ per unit (common knowledge). Demand D is uniform on [0, 100]. As the Supplier you carry the inventory risk: you produce the full quantity q up front, but only the units that meet demand, min(q, D), are paid for.
Supplier πS = w · E[min(q, D)] − PC · q
Retailer πR = (RP − w) · E[min(q, D)]

Private information

The Retail Price (RP) is the Retailer's private information: it can be 4€ or 5€, and the AI counterpart is not disclosing it. (Note: in this demo the AI has been assigned a RP of 4€ — it negotiates without revealing this.)

How the AI decides

shown for transparency — participants in the actual experiment do not see this
Benchmark The AI negotiates toward the Nash bargaining solution computed from its (undisclosed) Retail Price of 4€ and the common-knowledge Production Cost of 1€: a wholesale price of 2.80€ and 75 units, worth an expected profit of 56.25 to each side. That profit is its target.
Acceptance rule It accepts any offer — from chat or the interface — whose expected Retailer profit, (RP − w) · E[min(q, D)], is at least the target of 56.25. Accepted chat offers are re-posted in the interface as a binding offer for you to CONFIRM; accepted interface offers close the deal directly.
Counter-offer rules If your offer falls short, the AI diagnoses why and counters — and every counter-offer it makes is placed in the interface as its binding proposal:
  • Price too high (quantity workable): it keeps your quantity and offers the highest wholesale price that still reaches its target profit.
  • Quantity unworkable at your price: it keeps your price and offers the quantity that reaches its target while maximizing your expected profit.
  • Both unworkable, or no readable offer: it proposes the Nash benchmark offer itself (2.80€, 75 units).
Partial offers If your chat message names only a wholesale price, the AI completes it with the profit-maximizing quantity that meets its target; if it names only a quantity, the AI completes it with the corresponding price. Terms outside the allowed ranges (1–5€, 1–100 units) are rejected with a request to adjust.
Division of labor & validation These rules make every decision; the LLM only phrases the chat messages. Before a reply is shown, a rule-based validation wrapper evaluates it: any tangible offer the message cites must yield the AI an expected profit of at least its target, and decision messages must state the rule-computed terms exactly. Replies that fail are regenerated up to three times; after three unsuccessful attempts the draft whose cited offers carry the highest profit is sent (or a scripted message if none is usable). The LLM cannot accept, reject, or change an offer.
