I am a PhD candidate in Behavioral Operations at the [Rotterdam School of Management, Erasmus University Rotterdam](https://www.rsm.nl/). I am fortunate to be advised by prof. dr. Michael Becker-Peth and prof. dr. Jelle de Vries.

My dissertation, *Dealing with Conversational Agents*, studies how the rise of machine customers and AI-powered conversational agents reshapes operational decision-making. Using behavioral and field experiments, I examine how people negotiate with, delegate to, and behave after interacting with AI counterparts in B2B settings — and what this means for firms deciding when to automate the deal-making side of their operations.

Before the PhD, I completed an MSc in Business Analytics & Management at RSM, where my thesis on demand forecasting in grocery retail won the **Anton Dreesmann Award** for the best retail thesis in the Netherlands, and an Honours BSc in Economics & Business Economics at the University of Groningen.
